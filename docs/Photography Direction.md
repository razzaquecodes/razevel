# RAZEVÉL — Photography Direction

## Philosophy

RAZEVÉL photography is never commercial. It is never obvious. Every image should feel as though it belongs in a museum or a fashion editorial — not an online store.

The goal: **Make the viewer slow down.**

---

## The Three Pillars of RAZEVÉL Photography

### 1. Garment Photography — "The Monument"
**Setting:** Pure obsidian background, seamless  
**Lighting:** Single strong side source (key light only) with minimal fill  
**Mood:** Dramatic, still, monumental  
**Model Direction:** Neutral expression. Still posture. Eyes that do not seek approval.  
**Framing:** Full-length preferred. Leave significant breathing space.  
**Color Grading:** Deep blacks (crushed). Gold tones emphasized. Shadows rich and present.  
**References:** Paolo Roversi, Irving Penn, Guy Bourdin (for stillness)

### 2. Craft/Process Photography — "The Hand"
**Setting:** Workshop environment. Authentic, not styled.  
**Lighting:** Warm candlelight or single practical light source  
**Mood:** Intimate, reverent, documentary  
**Focus:** Hands. Fabric. Thread. Needle. Close-up macro.  
**Framing:** Extreme close-up. Fill the frame with craft.  
**Color Grading:** Warm amber tones. Deep shadows. Gold thread must catch light.  
**References:** Sebastião Salgado (for craft dignity)

### 3. Editorial/Bridal Photography — "The Moment"
**Setting:** Heritage architecture. Palaces. Havelis. Garden courtyards. Never studios.  
**Lighting:** Natural. Golden hour preferred. Soft overcast for intimacy.  
**Mood:** Contemplative. Serene. Timeless. Pre-cinematic.  
**Model Direction:** The person is in their own world. Not performing for the camera.  
**Film Style:** Shoot on film or grade to film. Visible grain acceptable.  
**Color Grading:** Muted, slightly desaturated. Champagne and ivory tones.  
**References:** Tim Walker, Raghubir Singh

---

## What We Never Do

- ❌ White or flat backgrounds (unless cream/ivory for product detail)
- ❌ Ecommerce-style flat lay product shots
- ❌ Forced smiles or posed expressions
- ❌ Busy or cluttered environments
- ❌ Fast, bright, high-contrast lifestyle photography
- ❌ Obvious studio flash lighting
- ❌ Watermarks on imagery
- ❌ Filters that reduce warmth or add cyan/teal

---

## Aspect Ratios & Cropping

| Format | Ratio | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | 16:9 or full bleed | Hero section |
| Portrait | 3:4 | Garment feature, story |
| Square | 1:1 | Journal thumbnail |
| Cinematic | 21:9 | Atelier/fullscreen sections |
| Detail | 1:1 macro | Craft close-ups |

---

## Post-Processing

- Always shoot RAW
- Color grade in Lightroom or DaVinci Resolve
- Preserve fabric texture — never over-smooth
- Gold embroidery must retain its sparkle and dimension
- Skin tones should be warm and natural, never overly processed
